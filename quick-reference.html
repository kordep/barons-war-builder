Barons' War — Quick Reference

Page 1 · The Round, Actions & Combat — 2nd Edition

Core Dice

Roll d10 ≥ stat to succeed. A natural 0 (10) always succeeds; a natural 1 always fails (and forbids Shield rolls).
  • −1 per Morale Penalty on Morale & Order checks.
  • Weary: −1 on all d10 rolls (Attack, Defence, Shield, Morale, Order). Does not affect the Strength-of-Charge d6.
  • Out of Coherency: −1 to Defence rolls.

The Round

1
Initiative: each player rolls d10, −1 per Retinue Leader Morale Penalty. Winner chooses who goes first; ties re-roll. Before rolling, you may spend up to 2 RL Actions for +1d10 each (pick highest).
2
Activation: players alternate activating Groups. Compulsory Activations first — Broken Groups, plus any Group an Ability subjects to Compulsory Activation (e.g. King John's Fear of Treachery). Then New Activations in any order. You may pass (not in Compulsory); 3 consecutive passes end the phase.
3
Housekeeping: ① check victory ② collect Action tokens ③ non-Weary Groups remove 1 Morale Penalty ④ Weary Groups: Morale check → pass removes 1 MP ⑤ remove Weary markers ⑥ ensure all Groups ≥1" apart.

Ranged Combat — Shoot

0
Move & Shoot (if weapon allows) — complete the move first.
1
Declare target — must be closest in range+LoS, else pass a Morale check.
2
Check LoS — centre of base to centre of base; see through own Group only. Only Warriors with LoS generate Attack dice. If ≥ half draw LoS through Hindering: −1 Attack per instance.
3
Measure range (edge to edge) — build Short-range and Long-range dice pools. Higher Ground (≥ half): +1 Attack.
4
Morale check if required — fail ends the combat.
5
Defender Reaction declared.
6
Generate pools & Resolve Attacks (see below). Casualties need LoS + range.

Melee Combat — Charge

1
Declare target — closest within LoS and potential Charge range (Movement + 6"), else Morale check.
2
Check LoS — straight line; cannot draw through any other Group or LoS-blocking / Impassable terrain.
3
Measure range — terrain modifies. Check Higher Ground (see p.2).
4
Morale Melee Modifier — compare Morale Penalties (table below).
5
Morale check(s) if required — fail ends the combat, no charge move.
6
Defender Reaction declared.
7
Strength of the Charge: +d6" to Movement. A 6 = +1 Attack die; a 1 = −1 Attack die.
8
Reaction Shooting resolves now (always Long range; all with LoS shoot; casualties don't fight, count for resolution).
9
Charge move — straight lines; every Warrior who can reach base contact must; Coherency not required. Failed Charge: nobody reaches → move full distance forward, stop 1" from any Group; combat ends (Reaction wasted).
10
Attack dice: 1d10 per Warrior in base contact or within 1" of a Group-mate in contact. Commanders: separate pool in contact (+1 die to Group pool if only within 1"). Warriors in contact with an enemy Commander may target him (separate pool, resolved first).

Resolve Attacks

1
Attack rolls — d10 ≥ Attack stat; weapon/terrain/Weary modify the score. Melee vs Broken: auto-hit.
2
Defence rolls — 1d10 per hit vs Defence (7+); Armour adds to score; Defend +2; Cover/Def-Position/Higher-Ground bonuses don't stack — pick one.
3
Shield rolls — failed Defence dice (not natural 1s): Small 9+ · Medium 8+ · Large 7+. Broken Groups can't.
4
Remove casualties — defender chooses (keep Coherency); Commander pools kill contact Warriors first.
5
Bonus Attacks resolve immediately.
6
Attack Back / Shoot Back — roles reversed; slain Warriors don't fight.

Combat Resolution

1
+1 Morale Penalty per casualty taken (all sources).
2
Victor = most casualties caused. Tie = draw. Melee with no casualties: attacker Forced Back 1". Melee draw: both Forced Back (attacker first); defender in Cover / Defensive Position is not.
3
Forced Back — 1" per casualty taken, along the attack line. Blocked by enemy / Building / Impassable / un-crossable Obstacle / a vertical drop (unless the Group chooses to Fall) = Trapped: +1 automatic casualty. Any base leaving the table is removed (whole Group off = destroyed, RL takes a MP).
4
Regroup (melee only) — winner takes a free Move; not on a draw, not after Defend.
5
Morale checks — see "When to Test" (p.2). Fail = Broken.

When to Test Morale

To avoid becoming Broken (Combat Resolution):

  • Lost the Combat and cumulative casualties ≥ half original Warriors.
  • Own Commander killed in the Combat (even if won).
  • Friendly Commander killed within 6".
  • Friendly Group wiped out within 6".

At any time: holding 6 Morale Penalties and required to take more; or when an Ability / weapon demands it (e.g. Fear — to Charge that Group; Close Ranks — the targeted infantry; a Lance's Shock — on taking casualties; over-riding a Compulsory Action).

Groups may use a friendly Commander's Morale if within Command range + LoS — for Morale checks only, never Order checks.

Broken · Rally · Weary

Broken

  • Immediately move full Movement away from the cause (or towards own table edge). Inside a Building: not moved — they hide.
  • No Reactions; melee attacks vs them auto-hit; Defence ok, no Shield rolls; no further Morale checks from Combat.
  • Any Warrior off the table = whole Group removed (RL takes a MP).

Rally (Compulsory)

  • Broken Groups must activate first and test Morale. Pass: Weary + take any Action immediately. Fail: Run towards own table edge.
  • Via Command Action (New Activations): Order check, then Morale check; failing the Rally then does nothing (no forced Run).

Weary

  • −1 on all d10 rolls. Causes: Run · declaring a 2nd Action/Reaction · Rallying · Abilities whose free attacks state it (Attack Back, Born for War, Ride Down).
  • Must pass a Morale check in Housekeeping to remove a Morale Penalty. All Weary markers removed at end of Housekeeping.

Commanders

  • Command range 6" + LoS (Banner 12" / Pennant 9", measured from the bearer). Friendlies draw LoS through the Command Group.
  • Groups in range may use the Commander's Morale (not for Order checks).
  • Command Action: target Group passes an Order check (own Morale) → gains a Reaction. From the Retinue Leader: an Action or Reaction. RL may use up to 2 Actions as Command Actions.
  • Max 1 Command Action per Group per round; Command Groups can't receive them from another Commander.
  • In Melee, a Commander may spend unused Actions: each = +1 Attack or Defence die.
  • Sudden Death: RL slain → another Commander must pass Morale to take over, or the game is lost immediately.

Coherency & LoS

  • Coherency: all bases wholly/partly within 2" (infantry) / 3" (cavalry) of a nominated Warrior. Required after Move/Run — not when Charging.
  • Out of Coherency: −1 Defence + compulsory Move to restore.
  • LoS: centre-to-centre; through own Group only; blocked by any other Group and LoS-blocking terrain.

  • Melee defender higher: +1 Attack and +1 Defence.

King John — Advanced

Chivalry & Prisoners

When a Chivalry Warrior is slain in Melee by another Chivalry Warrior/Group: d10 — 1–2 killed, 3+ taken prisoner (worth double VP). Chivalry Groups may always Charge enemy knights even if not closest.

Optional: Ranged Cap

Max ⅓ of the Retinue's points on ranged-armed Groups (Weapon Choice doesn't count).

Allied vs Combined Retinues

  • Allied: no shared Morale / Command Actions / CG upgrades across retinues; Coherency 15" to own RL.
  • Combined: share all Commander benefits; Coherency 15" to any RL.
  • Liege Lord rolls Initiative for the force. Mercenary RL is never Liege Lord; Outlaw retinues are Allied-only.
